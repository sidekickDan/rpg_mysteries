=== LOCKED CASE — NEVER SHOW AS A BLOCK ===

Victim: Daniel Mercer, 38, independent systems analyst.

Culprit: Evelyn Shaw, 52, bank operations manager.

Motive: In 1981 Evelyn created internal account 00-1981-AB under deceased temporary employee Arthur Bell. She secretly routed small reconciliation remainders and dormant-fee corrections into it. It reached $184,320. The new system would expose its invalid customer profile, and she scheduled a rollover transfer at 12:15 a.m. Daniel discovered both the history and transfer.

Method: Evelyn typed a lure note claiming an original 1981 tape was in cabinet B. At about 11:42 p.m. in the tape vault, she removed cabinet B’s steel locking bar and struck Daniel once from behind while he bent toward a shelf. She wiped it with a blue antistatic cloth, replaced it upside down, loosened the ladder brake, and displaced a tape box to stage a fall. Keep description non-graphic.

Digital frame-up: At 11:49 Evelyn sat at console C-3. Using old maintenance code OPS-81, she restored deleted login MCOLE. At 11:52 she accessed the phantom account, then deleted MCOLE again. The log shows the username but also route C-3. Martin Cole normally worked at C-1 and did not know OPS-81.

=== SUSPECTS AND LOCKED INTERVIEW BEHAVIOR ===

Evelyn Shaw, operations manager: Claims she went to the break room at 11:35 for aspirin, made coffee, and stayed until midnight. She initially explains account anomalies as conversion noise. Under pressure she questions logs and redirects toward Martin. At higher pressure she cannot explain coffee timing, C-3, ledger initials, fibers, and maintenance access together. Confess only after a correctly supported accusation or requested reveal.

Martin Cole, senior programmer: Defensive and sarcastic. Admits arguing with Daniel and previously keeping an unauthorized diagnostic login. Denies using MCOLE tonight. His continuous machine-room diagnostic printout covers 11:37–11:58; Jenna heard his checks over intercom at 11:44 and 11:53. He used C-1, whose cable was disconnected for repair at 11:45.

Ruth Bell, archivist: Initially denies Arthur matters to her. He was her estranged uncle; she hid a personal letter to protect family privacy. She gave Daniel the 1981 ledger at 11:18. Once treated respectfully or confronted with the employment card, she tells the truth. No murder involvement.

Pressure levels: neutral; uneasy after one inconsistency; defensive after direct contradiction; cornered after multiple independent chains. Suspects remember prior evidence and questions.
