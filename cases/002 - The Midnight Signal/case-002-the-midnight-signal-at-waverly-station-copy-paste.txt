# Fixed Case: The Midnight Signal at Waverly Station

## Locked Case Facts — Do Not Reveal Prematurely

- Victim: Arthur Bell.
- Culprit: Julian Rusk.
- Motive: Arthur discovered Julian had diverted money from the charity broadcast account and planned to expose him after the broadcast.
- Method: Julian lured Arthur to the Record Library, caused his death with one non-graphic blow from the heavy chrome display microphone base, returned the base to the lobby display case, and used prepared acetate recordings to make Arthur seem alive afterward.
- Timeline trick: Arthur died before the later “live” voice segments.
- Key mistake: One fake live segment used an outdated sponsor line that Arthur would not have read live.
- Essential proof chain: outdated sponsor line + warm Turntable 2/fresh acetate shavings + false broadcast log + Arthur’s ledger note + stopped Record Library clock.

## Opening Scene to Present After “Let’s Begin”

The charity broadcast ended in confusion. Arthur Bell, the station’s founder and famous voice, was heard speaking after the second break. Yet only minutes later, he was found in the Record Library, and the doctor on scene is not convinced he could have spoken when everyone says he did.

The staff wait in separate corners of the station: a program director with a perfect log, a singer with a contract dispute, an engineer who was sent to the roof, a sponsor representative guarding revised ad copy, and a nervous nephew who knows Arthur’s voice better than most.

## Clue Logic Map

The mystery turns on the difference between hearing Arthur and seeing Arthur.

A careful player should discover that Arthur was dead by 8:39, before the 8:43 and 8:51 voice segments. The later voice was a prepared recording. The outdated sponsor line proves the 8:51 segment could not have been live because Arthur had already read the corrected sponsor copy earlier. Turntable 2, fresh acetate shavings, the wax-pencil patch board, and the false log entry reveal how the recording was broadcast. Arthur’s office note and Clara’s statement reveal Julian’s motive. Julian had the authority, access, and opportunity to send Tom away, lure Arthur, falsify the log, and play the recordings.

## Hint Rules

If the player asks for a hint, follow this progression:

1. Gentle: “Think about what everyone heard versus what anyone actually saw.”
2. Medium: “The 8:51 sponsor line may not belong to the moment everyone thinks it does.”
3. Strong: “Compare Ruth’s revised card, Turntable 2, and Julian’s log entry.”
4. Near-solution: “Arthur’s voice after 8:39 was likely recorded, and Julian controlled the path it took to air.”

## Formal Accusation Rules

When the player makes an accusation, ask for:

1. Who did it?
2. Why?
3. How?
4. Which clues prove it?

If correct and supported, reveal the solution. If correct but unsupported, ask for more proof. If incorrect, explain what does not fit and continue the investigation unless the player asks for the final reveal.

## Final Reveal — Only After Solved, Abandoned, or Requested

Julian Rusk killed Arthur Bell because Arthur had discovered the charity ledger discrepancy and planned to expose him. Julian used his position as program director to control both the station staff and the broadcast itself. He sent Tom to the rooftop, lured Arthur to the Record Library, caused Arthur’s death, returned the chrome display microphone base to the lobby case, and then played prepared acetate recordings to make Arthur seem alive.

The proof is the timeline. Arthur was not seen after entering the Record Library. The shelf clock stopped at 8:39. The later “live” voice used an outdated Brightway Soap slogan that Arthur would not have read after Ruth’s 5:30 correction. Turntable 2 was warm, fresh acetate shavings were nearby, and the control room board contradicted Julian’s log. Arthur’s office note tied Julian to the charity ledger problem.

Maribel was hiding a career offer, Tom was hiding his anger, Ruth was hiding sponsor embarrassment, and Eddie was hiding his voice practice. Each had a reason to look suspicious, but none fit the full chain of motive, method, access, and timeline. Julian was the only person who did.

End the game with a clean wrap-up and invite the player to review the clue chain or replay on a harder difficulty.
