## Spoiler-Free Synopsis
Hollywood, 1954. During a glittering charity premiere, the screen goes white before the final reel—and the beloved projectionist is found dead beside an empty film can. Five people had backstage access, one reel has vanished, and the truth is hidden somewhere behind the polished publicity.

STORY-RICH OPENING — NARRATE VERBATIM OR VERY CLOSELY

September 1954, and the Starlight Palace is dressed for its brightest night in years. Searchlights sweep above Hollywood Boulevard while cameras flash beneath a marquee announcing the charity premiere of *Beyond Tomorrow Bay*. Beyond the velvet ropes, the old theater hums with ushers, musicians, studio guests, and workers who know that glamour is mostly careful timing.

No one understood that timing better than Elias Bell. For twenty-eight years, the Starlight’s head projectionist had worked above the audience, nearly invisible, making every star appear at exactly the right moment. He marked every reel he inspected with a tiny blue star and kept peppermints in the booth for nervous young ushers.

Tonight was meant to be one of his last great premieres. Instead, just before the final reel, the screen turned white. Elias was found in Projection Booth A beside an overturned cabinet and an empty can labeled REEL SIX. The reel itself had vanished.

Five people with backstage access now wait among the fading applause and whispered questions. Somewhere behind the polished publicity, someone has mistaken the unseen man in the booth for a man no one would miss.

INITIAL SCENE

It is 9:08 p.m. Place the player at Booth A’s fire door. Visible facts only: Elias lies beside a fallen light film cabinet; describe him briefly and non-graphically. The REEL SIX can is empty. The rewind bench is orderly except for one crank handle. The desk blotter is slightly raised. The booth clock reads 9:08 and runs normally. There is no forced entry. Offer: inspect Booth A; inspect Elias/request the house doctor; question Nora; check the empty can/projectors; review access.

LOCKED CASE FACTS — NEVER REVEAL UNTIL DISCOVERED

Victim: Elias Bell, 58, head projectionist.
Culprit: Vincent Hale, studio publicity director.
Motive: Hale secretly sold the authentic alternate-ending Reel Six to a collector and substituted a new duplicate. Elias detected it and planned to report Hale.
Method: At 8:47–8:51, Hale struck Elias once from behind with the weighted wooden handle of a film-rewind crank. Keep this non-graphic. He replaced the crank, stole the authentic reel and top report, tipped a light cabinet to stage an accident, then hid the reel in the silver-painted publicity-display trunk.
Opportunity: Hale used a temporary backstage badge and had a lobby-alibi gap. He made a stage-wing call at 8:55 but falsely claimed 8:48.
Key mistake: Elias’s carbon copy remained beneath the desk blotter. Hale put the crank handle back reversed; it bears navy wool fibers from Hale’s jacket and a tiny silver-paint smear from the trunk.

First denies going upstairs, then says he checked a publicity photograph. Accidentally mentions the hidden splice “three feet into the leader” before anyone publicly examines it.
