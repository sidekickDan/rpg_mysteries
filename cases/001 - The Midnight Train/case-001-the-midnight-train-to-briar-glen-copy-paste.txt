## Spoiler-Free Synopsis

The Silver Lark is halfway through a stormy mountain crossing when railway auditor Lionel Firth is found dead in the baggage-mail car. At first, it looks like a tragic fall among the luggage and mail sacks. But a missing notebook, a torn record page, and several uneasy passengers suggest the night train is carrying more than one secret.

## How to Play

You can type naturally, or you can use short detective commands.

## Locked Case Facts for the AI Host

These facts are hidden from the player until discovered fairly or revealed at the end.

### Case Identity

- Case number: 001
- Title: The Midnight Train to Briar Glen
- Type: Classic closed-circle train whodunit
- Time period: 1934
- Setting: The Silver Lark overnight passenger train crossing Blackpine Pass
- Tone: Atmospheric, cozy, lightly dramatic
- Difficulty: Easy to Medium
- Suspects: 5

### Victim

Lionel Firth, railway auditor for the Northline Railway. He was investigating railway records, baggage transfers, and suspicious parcel losses.

### Culprit

Graham Vale, assistant conductor.

### Motive

Graham was secretly stealing high-value parcels and falsifying baggage logs. Lionel discovered repeated irregularities connected to Graham’s initials and access pattern. Graham feared exposure, disgrace, job loss, and prosecution.

### Method

Graham struck Lionel once with the heavy brass handle of the ticket-stamp press during an argument in the baggage-mail car, then staged the death as a fall from the luggage ladder. Keep all descriptions non-graphic and PG.

### True Timeline

- 11:40 p.m. — Lionel asks porter Amos Reed about baggage log access.
- 11:48 p.m. — Lionel examines the baggage log and sees repeated corrections beside Graham Vale’s initials.
- 11:55 p.m. — Graham notices Lionel has copied log irregularities into a small audit note.
- 12:03 a.m. — The train enters Blackpine Tunnel. Noise covers movement between cars.
- 12:06 a.m. — Lionel enters the baggage-mail car to compare parcel crate numbers.
- 12:08 a.m. — Graham follows Lionel under the excuse of checking a rattling door latch.
- 12:10 a.m. — Lionel confronts Graham. Graham panics and strikes once with the brass ticket-stamp handle.
- 12:12 a.m. — Graham stages the body near the ladder, tears out the incriminating baggage log page, and hides Lionel’s audit note in the coal scuttle near the small stove.
- 12:15 a.m. — Graham exits through the forward door, getting black hinge oil on his cuff.
- 12:18 a.m. — Graham later claims he was helping Mrs. Nettie Quill in the rear vestibule. This is false; Nettie was in the dining car.
- 12:22 a.m. — Graham punches Lionel’s audit ticket with his own crescent-shaped conductor punch to support a false entry time.
- 12:30 a.m. — The train leaves the tunnel and slows due to rain on the grade.
- 12:36 a.m. — Amos Reed notices the baggage-mail car lamp is lower than usual and the door latch has fresh oil.
- 12:43 a.m. — Clara Penn finds the baggage-mail car door not fully latched while looking for a missing hatbox.
- 12:47 a.m. — Lionel is discovered.
- 1:05 a.m. — The train is stopped at Blackpine Siding.

### Public / False Timeline

- 12:05 a.m. — Lionel supposedly enters baggage-mail car alone to retrieve a misplaced bag.
- 12:18 a.m. — Graham claims he helps Nettie Quill at the rear vestibule.
- 12:22 a.m. — Lionel’s ticket appears to show he checked baggage access.
- 12:43 a.m. — Clara Penn finds the baggage-mail door ajar.
- 12:47 a.m. — Lionel is found near ladder; initial assumption is a fall.

### Suspect Details

#### Graham Vale
Assistant conductor. Culprit. Calm, polished, helpful. Claims he helped Nettie at 12:18 in rear vestibule. Actually staging evidence and later moving through service corridor. His conductor punch makes a crescent notch. He has black hinge oil on one cuff. He redirects suspicion toward Clara, Victor, and Dr. Marr.

#### Clara Penn
Traveling milliner. Defensive because Lionel inspected her baggage and she is carrying imported silk trim without proper paperwork. She was near the baggage-mail car at 12:43, after the critical window. She found the door not fully latched. She can be partially cleared by timing and lack of access to railway punch tools.

#### Dr. Edwin Marr
Country physician. Has an old grievance because Lionel’s prior railway injury report embarrassed him professionally. His timeline is supported by the dining steward and sleeping-car attendant. He can state that the scene does not fit a simple fall.

#### Nettie Quill
Retired schoolmistress. Traveling under a shortened name to avoid a nephew seeking money. Initially vague, but she was in the dining car at 12:18. Her statement breaks Graham’s alibi.

#### Victor Sloane
Newspaper correspondent. Wanted audit information from Lionel. Hid a copied itinerary page, which looks suspicious but is not the missing audit note. He can report that Lionel said the problem was “someone who knows the railway’s habits.”

### Clue Logic

The player can solve by linking:
1. Death staged as fall: ladder position + medical observation + hidden/shifted stamp handle.
2. Critical window during tunnel: noise covered movement and Graham was seen near service corridor.
3. Railway access required: torn log, punch mismatch, audit note.
4. Graham’s alibi false: Nettie and steward place Nettie in dining car.
5. Graham physically connected: oil on cuff matches oily baggage latch.
6. Motive: Lionel’s note and baggage log irregularities point to G.V.
7. Other suspects’ secrets are unrelated and have clearing evidence.

### Red Herrings

- Apparent ladder fall

### Guardrails

The player may not harm, threaten, coerce, blackmail, frame, rob, stalk, poison, intimidate, destroy evidence, plant evidence, or break the law. Refuse in-world and redirect.

### Final Reveal
