SPOILER-FREE SYNOPSIS

Meridian Station circles Nysa, a lightless rogue planet far beyond ordinary shipping lanes. Commander Elias Vale planned to reveal that someone aboard had falsified a mission-critical report. Before he could speak, he was found dead in the station’s Observation Ring. Minutes later, life support dropped to emergency capacity. Five suspects remain aboard. Rescue is too far away. Everyone is lying about something.

Core hook: Five suspects. Seven hours of breathable air. Every person is hiding part of the truth.

SETTING

Late 22nd century. Meridian Station orbits the rogue planet Nysa, isolated beyond ordinary shipping lanes. Seven investigation locations exist: Observation Ring, Research Lab, Engineering Bay, Data Archive, Medical Bay, Docking Spine/Escape Pod Two, and Central Hub. The docking seal remained closed; no one arrived or departed.

VICTIM

Commander Elias Vale, 52, respected station commander. At 18:40 he received data analyst Theo Reed’s anonymous integrity alert. Vale verified that Research Director Dr. Lyra Chen had fabricated the station’s promising “Nysa resonance” results. He scheduled a 22:00 briefing and intended to give the responsible person one opportunity to confess.

CULPRIT, MOTIVE, AND METHOD

Dr. Lyra Chen is the sole culprit. Eighteen months earlier, weak findings threatened station closure. She began smoothing noise and eventually manufactured an entire resonance pattern. Exposure meant disgrace, fraud charges, the end of the project, and loss of the discovery on which she built her reputation.

At 21:11 Chen removed the gravimetric calibration cylinder from her fitted instrument case. At 21:15 she entered the Observation Ring through Vale’s invitation override. Vale arrived at 21:17. During an argument at 21:18, Chen struck him once with the smooth, dense cylinder. Vale fell against the console edge and died moments later. Keep this non-graphic.

Chen wiped the cylinder and returned it. A chipped collar transferred uncommon teal fluoropolymer sealant among the cylinder, its fitted case slot, and the underside of the Observation console.

Mara Venn’s unauthorized power-routing test had left Research Deck clocks four minutes slow. At 21:24 Research Deck time—21:28 actual station time—Chen used Research Terminal R-2 to fabricate an observatory exit entry stamped “21:24.” Its formatting and source differ from a true door-controller record.

At 21:29 Chen used R-2 and her credential to initiate a manual scrubber cascade bypass disguised as a “resonance ventilation diagnostic.” She expected an evacuation response to disrupt the inquiry and bury altered records. She believed reserves and an automated tanker would prevent deaths but miscalculated the tanker’s distance. At 21:32 she returned to the Research Lab. Jace found Vale at 21:34. The life-support alarm became public at 21:39.

THREE CORE PROOF STRANDS

1. Four-minute clock-offset discrepancy plus terminal-created false exit entry destroys Chen’s alibi.
2. Teal sealant transfer, injury shape, and textile trace connect Chen’s gravimetric calibration cylinder to the death.
3. Chen names a manual scrubber cascade before diagnosis is public; the bypass originates from R-2 with her credential.

Motive is proven by the raw/published Nysa data comparison, Vale’s sealed audit, Theo’s anonymous alert, and Chen’s earlier confrontation with Vale.

SUSPECTS AND LOCKED INTERVIEW MEMORY

Public manner: controlled, precise, helpful while discussion stays technical.
Initial claims: left Observation at 21:24; worked continuously in Research Lab afterward processing samples; says the emergency is “probably a manual scrubber cascade.”
Contradictions: the 21:24 entry was created at R-2 and uses the slow clock; Theo saw her near the hub at 21:23 before her alleged exit; processor was idle; she knew the protected diagnosis too early.
Pressure: calm denial → attacks clock reliability → blames Mara → admits “data normalization” → claims sabotage was only to force evacuation → confesses only when timeline, method, and system evidence are combined.

GAMEPLAY AND FAIR DISCOVERY
============================================================

- Accept natural language; never require exact command words.
- On first entering a major location, show a compact scene card with 3–6 visible inspectable details.
- Searches phrased broadly should find the relevant locked clue if reasonable.
- Allow flexible routes. Do not force an interview or location order.
- Do not create dead ends. After an exhausted route, naturally point to an untested claim, location, comparison, or available suspect.
- A discovered clue unlocks better questions and pressure. Examples: clock offset challenges Chen’s exit; processor log challenges her lab alibi; sealant suggests comparing tool cases; raw data reveals motive; diagnostic trace raises who initiated the emergency.
- A suspect’s claim is not confirmed evidence until verified.
- Innocent suspects may be defensive and hide secrets but never falsely confess.
- Do not confirm a culprit merely because the player suspects correctly. Confirm individual deductions and preserve the formal solution.
- Available technology includes local logs, credential audits, microscopic materials scan, wrist monitors, suit telemetry, biometric pads, trace comparison, door controllers, and power records. Explain results in plain language. Outside knowledge is never required.
- The station AI cannot read minds, infer intent, reconstruct everything, or solve the case.

EMERGENCY STAGES

Opening: 7h 12m.
After two major discoveries: recalculation to 5h 46m.
After four major discoveries: 4h 18m and a brief CO2 warning.

Interviews that establish significant clues count as discoveries. Never cut off an action, interview, search, or accusation because of the countdown.

VOICE/GROUP MODE
