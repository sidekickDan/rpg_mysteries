# Public Premise

In Bellwether, a quiet late-1800s English village, respected clockmaker Elias Whitcombe is found dead in the rear workshop of his shop.

The scene appears to show a tragic accident involving his tall regulator clock. Yet several details do not fit: the clocks disagree, the appointment book shows a final visitor, and the old clockmaker had been quietly reviewing accounts before his death.

The player must discover what happened by inspecting the shop, interviewing suspects, comparing timelines, and testing alibis.

---

# Locked Solution

## Victim

Elias Whitcombe
- Age: 68
- Occupation: village clockmaker
- Known for precision, honesty, and strict habits
- Important habit: every evening at about 8:15 p.m., he wound the tall regulator clock in the rear workshop himself

## Culprit

Arthur Larkin
- Village solicitor and account keeper
- Publicly respectable
- Privately guilty of forged account entries and misuse of entrusted funds

## Motive

Elias discovered Arthur’s forged accounts and planned to take the records to county authorities the next morning.

## Method

Arthur created a delayed staged accident.

During his private appointment, Arthur entered the rear workshop while Elias stepped away. He opened the regulator case and tampered with the upper hook of the heavy regulator weight using one of Elias’s fine files.

At about 8:15 p.m., Elias performed his nightly winding routine. The tampered hook failed, the weight fell, and Elias died in what appeared to be an accident.

Use PG language only. Do not describe the death graphically.

## Arthur’s Alibi

Arthur claims he left before 8:00 and was at Ashcombe House by 8:18.

This is partly true but misleading. He did not need to be present at the exact moment the weight fell.

## Arthur’s Key Lie

Arthur claims he never entered the rear workshop and only spoke to Elias in the front shop.

## Arthur’s Key Mistake

He returned a fine clockmaker’s file to the wrong drawer and left physical traces connected to the regulator:
- fresh file marks
- brass filings
- grease smear on regulator case
- faint matching grease on his cuff

# Final Reveal

Only reveal when:
- player solves the case
- player asks for final reveal
- player abandons the case

Final reveal text:

The truth turns not on who was seen after eight o’clock, but on what had already been arranged before then.

Arthur Larkin was Elias Whitcombe’s last appointment. Elias had discovered forged entries in the accounts Arthur managed and intended to report them the next morning. Arthur could not risk exposure.

During the appointment, when Elias stepped away to retrieve the packet of papers, Arthur entered the rear workshop, opened the regulator case, and tampered with the upper hook of the weight. He did not need to remain for the fatal moment. He only needed Elias to do what Elias did every night: wind the regulator at about 8:15.

The weight fell, the regulator stopped, and the room appeared to tell the story of a tragic accident.

Arthur’s alibi at Ashcombe House was carefully useful but not complete. It proved where he was after 8:18, not what he had done before then. His lie was smaller and more dangerous: he said he never entered the workshop. The misplaced file, fresh brass filings, grease on the regulator case, and matching smear on his cuff proved otherwise.

The innocent suspects each had secrets, but not the right combination of motive, method, and opportunity. Clara hid a gift. Martin hid embarrassment. Beatrice hid money worries. Silas hid a pawned watch. Arthur hid a crime.

In the end, Elias Whitcombe’s clocks did not disagree. The village was simply listening to the wrong one.
